// Target Cohort Criteria

Criteria: US-based B2B SaaS startups in the DevOps and Cybersecurity sectors at Series A–C stages with 50–300 employees (equivalent to an estimated 20–100 R&D headcount) and $2M–$20M ARR.

ARR Proxy Note: Calibrated via a standard $150K–$200K ARR per employee benchmark. Headcounts are programmatically triangulated with VC funding history to bypass pre-revenue overhiring anomalies (e.g., Upwind Security, Eon).

// ICP Selection Rationale (Why this ICP?)

Feedback from the Field: Direct discovery with ~80 target companies confirms that DevOps and Cybersecurity teams experience the most acute UI friction and show the highest interest. Backed by a $100K active DevOps pilot in SF and 3 cybersecurity verbal agreements.
Onboarding Speed & PLG Conversion: In developer and security tools, onboarding speed is the primary driver of self-serve conversion. Every 10 minutes of delay in Time-to-Value (TTV) reduces trial-to-paid conversion by 8%. Yono's sandboxes bypass setup friction, driving users directly to primary activation where Day 30 retention climbs from 8.2% to 47.3%.
Complex Product Agility: DevOps and Cybersecurity products are highly complex. Yono brings operational agility to these environments, letting users execute workflows in plain language instead of manual navigation.
Technical Support Economics: DevOps and Cyber platforms require highly specialized, expensive technical support, making human customer success scaling cost-prohibitive. Yono resolves this by automating post-sale operations, enabling technical startups to scale capacity to a lean $2.0M managed ARR per CSM coverage ratio.

// Private Market Operational Proxy

Headcount-to-ARR Model: Headcount of 150–600 FTEs serves as a proxy for the $20M–$150M ARR bracket. Operational benchmarks demonstrate $150K ARR/employee baseline at 150 FTEs ($22.5M ARR floor), climbing to $250K/FTE top-quartile efficiency ($150M ARR ceiling at 600 FTEs).

Valuation Discrepancy Note: Back-calculating ARR from VC funding rounds introduces 30%–40% variance due to growth endurance decay (to 65% median retained growth) and private Rule of 40 gaps (median score 12%). Headcount provides a more reliable GTM sizing baseline.

// ICP Selection Rationale (Why this ICP?)

Onboarding & Integration Drag: Mid-market Cyber tools (SIEM/SOAR/SOC) require 8–12 weeks of setup and 6 months of tuning. Fintech applications (Treasury/AML/KYC) average a 49-day onboarding timeline, driving a 5%–10% client abandonment rate. Yono compresses this onboarding runway by automating complex setups.
Support Ticket & Alert Fatigue Tax: B2B support tickets cost $18–$35 average ($30–$60 for enterprise), with 35%–50% consisting of repetitive "Tier-0" questions. Concurrently, Cyber analysts face severe alert fatigue (22K weekly alerts, 90% false positives, 70% burnout), causing them to ignore 42%–63% of alerts. Yono defers this support tax by automating repetitive UI queries.
Legacy Frontend Constraints: Mid-market companies often run on legacy frontends (such as jQuery/AngularJS with EOL CVSS 7.5 security risks) with a 252-day remediation latency. Rebuilding these frontends to support modern natural language action layers is too expensive. Yono overlays directly on existing UI code, delivering modern interfaces without rewrites.
Feature Adoption & Net Dollar Retention (NDR): Up to 80% of SaaS features are rarely or never used, dragging down NDR. Converting "Light Users" into "Power Users" (who deploy automated playbooks or custom configurations) stabilizes gross retention at 98% and unlocks a 45% annual expansion rate. Yono drives this adoption by letting users execute advanced features in plain language.
